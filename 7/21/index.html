                          <July 21st>

                          Inherit the Wind (1960)

                          July 21st 1925 - John Scopes is found guilty of teaching evolution

                          Teacher B.T. Cates is arrested for teaching Darwin's theories. Famous lawyer Henry Drummond defends him; fundamentalist politician Matthew Brady prosecutes. This is a very thinly disguised rendition of the 1925 "Scopes monkey trial" with debates between Clarence Darrow and William Jennings Bryan taken largely from the transcripts.

                          Inherit the Wind (1999)

                          July 21st 1925 - John Scopes is found guilty of teaching evolution

                          Based on a real-life case in 1925, two great lawyers argue the case for and against a science teacher accused of the crime of teaching evolution.

                          Inherit the Wind (1988)

                          July 21st 1925 - John Scopes is found guilty of teaching evolution

                          A teacher has taught about evolution in a small town in Tennessee. He has violated state law by doing this. Two great lawyers appear, one to assist in his defense, the other to help prosecute him. The town is divided in many ways which become apparent as the trial progresses. This is a dramatization of the Scopes Monkey Trial that pitted William Jennings Bryan against Clarence Darrow in a real case.
